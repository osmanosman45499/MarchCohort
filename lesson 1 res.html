What is xG in Football

Put simply, Expected Goals (xG) is a metric designed to measure the probability of a shot resulting in a goal.

An xG model uses historical information from thousands of shots with similar characteristics to estimate the likelihood of a goal on a scale between 0 and 1.

Deeper dive in to XG

  1. A shot with an xG value of 0.2 is one that we would generally expect to be converted twice in every 10 attempts.
  2. Expected Goals (xG) is a metric that's intended to measure the probability of a shot resulting in a goal
  3. xG is calculated using a serious deep dive into the past. To measure the likelihood of shots being converted into actual goals
  4. xG uses historical information from thousands of shots with similar characteristics to estimate how likely a goal is on a scale between 0 and 1.

Benefits of XG in Football

  • xG shows us the importance of shot quality by emphasising the low chance of scoring from disadvantaged positions.
  • It allows coaches and their backroom staff to identify particularly good finishers.
  • Expected Goals also highlights the fact that crosses aren't the most effective way of scoring.
  • xG is also great for team analysis.
  • Expected Goals can also be extremely useful for scouting purposes.
